2. Leveling up System:
The game is open to anyone who has access to the download, so Web2 players can play the game, but cannot participate on a Web3/blockchain level and have only 4 characters available to them. We want them to experience the gameplay and understand the benefits of purchasing a ChainBoi and getting involved in the Web3 side of the game.
When a player mints a ChainBoi, the ChainBoi is considered a “trainee”. The holder can play the game with four characters & 4 weapons. The holder can also acquire $BATTLE tokens through in-game play and sell them for $AVAX and also buy assets (guns, armor, loot boxes) on our website. But they do not have the advantages of winning prizes through tournaments, receiving airdrops, (We also want to keep newly minted players options limited so that they have a large incentive to level-up a ChainBoi and obtain the benefits in doing so e.g. tournaments for prizes, airdrops, and owning assets.)
ChainBois can then progress through 7 levels
Leveling up is done by paying a certain amount of $AVAX. This is done on our website by going to the “Training Room” page which will (when wallet is connected) show the ChainBoi/s that the player owns. Each ChainBoi will have a “level-up” button underneath him, when the “level-up” button is clicked on the website the holder will be directed to sign a transaction and pay the $AVAX. The ChainBoi then goes through an  animated transformation and acquires its corresponding badge in the upper left of the NFT. The ChainBoi is then “leveled-up” and can participate in the corresponding tournament and $BATTLE token airdrops.
In game, when a ChainBoi levels up they acquire 4 new characters.
As stated, leveling-up also unlocks access to higher level tournaments offering reflectively higher prizes for the top three ChainBois who have acquired the most points from in-game play that week.
At any time during the week, a holder can collect their $BATTLE tokens on blockchain by signing a transaction using points accrued in game. This gives them the option to purchase new weapons/armor/loot boxes during the course of the week, sell $BATTLE tokens, or hold onto their points in order to win the current tournament.

The scoreboard will display the tournament level and the current live score of the top 10 ChainBois as well as a countdown clock to signify when the weekly tournament is ending. # ChainBois Web2 Player Onboarding Implementation Plan

## Overview

This document outlines the implementation strategy for onboarding traditional Web2 players to ChainBois, a third-person shooter game built on the AVAX blockchain. A partnership with Ava Labs would seek to create a seamless entry point for non-crypto users while maintaining the value and exclusivity of the original ChainBois NFT collection.

## Implementation Strategy

### User Entry Flow

1. When launching the game, users will be presented with two options:  
     
   - Play as a Web3 Player (using their wallet and purchased ChainBois NFT)  
   - Play as a Normie (Web2 player without crypto experience)

   

2. For Normie players, the system will:  
     
   - Generate a platform-managed wallet automatically  
   - Mint and assign a restricted "Normie NFT" to their wallet  
   - Create game credentials linked to this wallet

   

3. The platform-managed wallet will:  
     
   - Store the user's NFT and any earned in-game tokens  
   - Manage transactions on behalf of the user  
   - Operates similarly to a custodial exchange wallet  
   - Does not require users to manage private keys or understand blockchain operations

### Normie NFT Specifications

#### Restrictions for Normie NFTs:

- Restricted from participating in high-level tournaments (limited to Trainee-level tournaments only)  
- Receive 50% reduced $BATTLE token airdrops compared to Web3 NFTs  
- Cannot be transferred out of the managed wallet  
- Limited to basic characters and starter weapons  
- Cannot purchase or trade assets on secondary markets  
- Metadata will include `"normie": true` flag to distinguish from full Web3 NFTs  
- Limited to 4 characters (as mentioned in the feature doc for Web2 players)  
- Cannot progress beyond "Trainee" level without upgrading to full Web3 status

#### Technical Implementation:

- NFT smart contract will include conditional functions that check for the `normie` status  
- Tournament eligibility, airdrops, and asset transfers will verify NFT status before processing  
- In-game stats (kills, scores) will be recorded in the NFT metadata regardless of status

### Normie to Web3 Upgrade Process

1. Normie players can upgrade to full Web3 status through the following process:  
     
   - Access the "Upgrade" section in the game interface  
   - Pay a fee of 120% of the current floor price of the Web3 collection  
   - Choose to either:  
     - Create their own Web3 wallet and transfer the upgraded NFT  
     - Continue using the platform wallet with full privileges

   

2. Upon upgrade:  
     
   - The `normie` flag is removed from metadata  
   - All restrictions are lifted  
   - The NFT gains full mobility and transfer rights  
   - Player gains access to all tournaments and full airdrops  
   - All accumulated game stats and progress remain intact

### Normie NFT Supply Management

#### Recommended Approach:

- Create an initial pool of 1,000 Normie NFTs  
- When a Normie player upgrades to Web3 status, use 20% of their upgrade fee to sweep the floor and purchase an existing Web3 NFT  
- Convert this purchased NFT to Normie status (add `normie: true` flag)  
- Return it to the Normie NFT pool  
- This creates a sustainable cycle that:  
  - Prevents dilution of the original collection  
  - Maintains floor price support  
  - Provides liquidity to existing holders  
  - Ensures a constant supply of Normie NFTs for new players

Chainbois’ Game

Summary:
This document would cover the game's roadmaps, strategies, mechanics, revenue, asset supply, release, and sale techniques.

Weapons:
Mode of Release: Seasonal Battlepasses

Seasons: From the game’s launch onwards, season-specific themed periods, preferably a month, would feature new content to be released into the game and add to its excitement.

Battlepass: A battlepass would include any new item to be introduced into the game, be it, weapons, characters, operator skills (if applicable), boosters, and whatnot.
Free Battlepass: A free battlepass would come with each new season, and every item included in the battlepass can be viewed in the free battlepass but only the free-to-unlock items remain accessible until the premium battlepass is purchased.
Premium Battlepass: A paid battlepass package that unlocks ownable assets like higher-rarity weapon blueprints other than the base gun model released in the free battlepass. A premium battlepass purchase grants NFTs of the introduced higher-rarity assets (weapons, characters, asset skins, etc) to the gamer. The gamer can sell these on the secondary market, but only a holder of such an asset during the login session can use the corresponding in-game asset.

Rarity: All weapons, upon release, would have free and unlockable base models in the free battlepass, but would include a single higher rarity in the premium battlepass of the season it's released in.
Blueprints: Each gun model would have a base blueprint, then higher-rarity blueprints equivalent to an order such as this:
Base -> Epic -> Legendary -> Mythic (With Legacy having the same rarity as Legendary but with a much lesser release probability)
Base Blueprints: These are the actual gun models without any extra aesthetics.
Epic Blueprints: These are customized blueprints of the base model with a separate color system, and may or may not include any of the following:
Custom Iron sight
Custom Reload
Custom Gun Shots/Firing sounds
Unlocked from Premium Battlepasses, purchased through special events, and Apex Loot boxes…
Legendary/Legacy Blueprints: These are customized blueprints of the base model that would have special effects, such as special kill effects. It may or may not include any of the following:
Custom Iron sight
Custom Reload
Custom Gun Shots/Firing sounds
Reactiveness: Some of the blueprints in this category may have a change in appearance depending on the kill count of its user
Purchased through seasonal bundles, and Apex Loot boxes…
Mythic Blueprints: These are the highest rarity blueprint any gun model can have, with gun models only capable of possessing a single mythic blueprint.
Mythics would bring additional features to a gun like none of the lesser rarities can, for instance:
Digital Sights
Custom reloads
Custom gun inspections
Custom kill effects: A base kill effect that comes with the Level 0 Mythic upon purchase, an advanced kill effect unlocked upon upgrading the mythic to Level 2, and a final kill effect at Level 4, with each change in flare significantly noticeable from the previous one
Custom attachments (If applicable): Select attachments for the gun model would have a custom redesign that takes effect when equipped on a Mythic blueprint.
Mythic Blueprints would be purchasable with $Battle Tokens, with the price changing to the market. Upgrades, however, would be carried out through a different mechanism.
Mythic Upgrades: Upgrades would carried out using upgrade chips in-game or on the website. Upgrade chips would not be directly purchasable using $Battle tokens, rather they’ll drawn against for a fixed amount of $Battle tokens. That is:
An Upgrade bundle may require 10K $Battle tokens for a single draw at it with these odds such as these:
50% chance to draw 10 Upgrades chips
25% chance to draw 20 Upgrades chips
15% chance to draw 30 Upgrades chips
6% chance to draw 40 Upgrades chips
3% chance to draw 60 Upgrade chips
1% chance to draw 100 Upgrade chips
Then to upgrade a Mythic from Level 0 through 5 would require this many Upgrade Chips:
Level 0 -> 1 - 150 Chips
Level 1 -> 2 - 250 (400) Chips
Level 2 -> 3 - 500 (900) Chips
Level 3 -> 4 - 700 (1600) Chips
Level 4 -> 5 - 900 (2500) Chips
It won't matter if users get to pull on the higher odds, there’ll be no short supply of those pulling on the lower odds.
Mythic Unlock:
Level 0: Custom Reload, Digital Sight (Base), Custom gun inspection, Custom firing sounds, Bullet trail (if applicable.
Level 1:  Custom Attachments
Level 2: Advanced Kill Effect
Level 3: Advanced Blueprint Variant (With Advanced Digital Sight)
Level 4: Final Kill Effect
Level 5: Final Blueprint Variant (With Final Digital Sight)
