## What does a player do in a typical session?

Player launches the game and selects from seven modes — Battle Royale, Team Deathmatch, Kill Confirmed, Gun Fight, Capture the Flag, Search and Destroy, or Frontline. They pick a character and weapon loadout, then enter a match across one of three landscapes. During gameplay they earn points based on kills, objectives, and match performance. After the match, they go to the Armory page on the website where their points balance is displayed. They can convert points directly to $BATTLE tokens on-chain at a dynamic exchange rate that adjusts based on the health of the rewards wallet. With $BATTLE tokens they buy weapon NFTs, armor NFTs, or loot boxes — all transferred directly to their wallet and immediately usable in-game. They can visit the Training Room to level up their ChainBoi NFT by paying AVAX, which updates the NFT metadata on-chain and unlocks new characters, higher tournament tiers, and increased airdrop eligibility. During tournament weeks, match points feed a live leaderboard on the Battleground page — when the tournament ends, prizes are automatically distributed to the top three players' wallets via on-chain transactions with no manual claiming required. Between tournaments, players strategize: upgrade their ChainBoi level, buy better weapons, or hold tokens. The cycle repeats weekly.

## What does blockchain add?

Three specific things blockchain enables that a traditional server cannot. First, true asset ownership — weapon NFTs, armor NFTs, and ChainBoi NFTs all live in the player's wallet. When a player buys a weapon in the Armory using $BATTLE tokens, that weapon is an on-chain NFT they can use in-game or sell on any Avalanche marketplace for real value. In a traditional game, that weapon would be locked to their account with no transferability or resale. Second, verifiable on-chain progression — when a player levels up their ChainBoi NFT in the Training Room, the level, badge, and progression data are written directly to the NFT metadata on-chain. Their game history and achievements are provably theirs, permanent, and portable — not sitting on a company database that could be wiped or shut down. Third, trustless prize distribution — weekly tournament AVAX prizes are automatically distributed to winner wallets via on-chain transactions when the tournament ends. No manual payouts, no collect buttons, no trust required — prizes land directly in the winners' wallets, fully verifiable on Snowtrace.

## What keeps players coming back?

The eight-tier NFT leveling system (Private through Field Marshal) creates long-term progression — each level unlocks new characters in-game, access to higher tournament tiers with bigger AVAX prizes, and larger $BATTLE token airdrop allocations, giving players a continuous reason to invest in their ChainBoi. Weekly tournaments with live leaderboards on the Battleground page and real AVAX payouts create recurring competitive stakes. The core strategic tension is the choice between holding points to win the current tournament or converting them to $BATTLE tokens in the Armory to purchase weapon and armor NFTs that could give a competitive edge in future tournaments — this makes every match consequential beyond just the gameplay. The weapon system spans thirty weapons across eight categories with a four-tier blueprint rarity system (Base, Epic, Legendary, Mythic), and the Mythic upgrade path using upgrade chips drawn through a probability-based system gives collectors and completionists deep long-term goals. Seasonal battlepasses introduce new weapons, characters, and rewards monthly. Loot boxes with transparent probability tiers in the Armory provide randomized rewards and excitement. Multiple airdrop types — based on NFT level, NFT traits, and token holdings — reward different styles of engagement. And because every weapon and armor piece is a tradeable NFT, time spent playing has tangible financial value through secondary market sales on the Inventory page.

## Describe your in-game economy

Token emission is controlled through a calibrated points-to-token conversion ratio — acquiring a Legendary blueprint requires roughly the equivalent of 1,000+ games of earned tokens, and a Mythic requires 10,000+, ensuring inflation is tied to real gameplay investment. On the sink side, when players spend $BATTLE tokens in the Armory on weapons, armor, loot boxes, or Mythic upgrade chip draws, the burn-to-recycle split is automatically determined by the system based on the health of the rewards wallet — no manual tuning required. The system evaluates five health tiers in real-time: at ABUNDANT (75%+ of initial supply remaining), 50% of tokens are burned permanently and 50% recycled to the rewards wallet; at HEALTHY (50-75%), the burn drops to 40%; at MODERATE (30-50%), 30%; at SCARCE (15-30%), 20%; and at CRITICAL (below 15%), only 10% is burned to preserve the prize economy. This fully automated, self-regulating mechanism runs on a 6-hourly sweep cycle and ensures the economy stays sustainable without any manual intervention. NFT supply is fixed — 4,000 ChainBoi NFTs with no additional minting beyond the initial collection. Weapon NFTs are released in controlled quantities through seasonal battlepasses with defined rarity tiers, preventing oversupply. An Agent Pool buyback system allows the platform to repurchase weapon NFTs from players at fixed prices using $BATTLE tokens and then reintroduce them at higher prices, regulating secondary market supply and maintaining asset value. Level-up costs are paid in AVAX, not $BATTLE tokens, creating a separate revenue stream — 75% funds tournament prize pools and 25% supports ongoing development. This means tournament prizes are funded by ecosystem growth rather than token printing, keeping the token economy and prize economy structurally independent.

## Who is your primary player?

We're targeting both traditional competitive shooter fans and crypto-native gamers, with the UX designed to serve both without friction. Web2 players download the game and play immediately — no wallet, no crypto, no sign-up barrier. They get a starter set of characters and weapons and full access to all seven game modes. The blockchain layer is invisible to them until they choose to engage. As they play, they see what NFT holders access — the full character roster, weapon purchases in the Armory, tournament prizes on the Battleground, real asset ownership in the Inventory — and the upgrade path is presented naturally through the website's mint flow with wallet connection guidance. For crypto-native players, the experience is natively Web3 from the start — wallet connection on the landing page, NFT minting, Training Room level-ups with on-chain transactions, $BATTLE token earning and spending, secondary market trading from the Inventory page, and verifiable tournament payouts. The design principle is that blockchain enhances the experience for those who opt in but never blocks access for those who haven't yet — the game is the onboarding, not the wallet.
